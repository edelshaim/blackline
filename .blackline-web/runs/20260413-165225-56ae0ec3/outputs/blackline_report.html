Appendix [X] — Proposed Service Commitment Framework

 

Power Wood Canada Corp. v Canadian National Railway Company

CTA Case No. 26-05910

 

 

1. Status and Legal Character

 

1.1 This Proposed Service Commitment Framework (the “Framework”) is filed by Power Wood Canada Corp. (“Power Wood”) as Appendix [X] to its Reply in CTA Case No. 26-05910. It constitutes a formal undertaking by Power Wood with respect to the Traffic (within the meaning of s. 116(1.2)(d) of the Canada Transportation Act, SC 1996, c 10 (the “CTA”),”)), and is offered in support of, and as consistent with, a level-of-service determination under ss. 113–116 CTA and, in the alternative, a shipper undertaking under s. 169.31 CTA.

 

1.2 The operative clauses that follow are drafted so that they may be reflected in an Agency order directing Canadian National Railway Company (“CN”) to provide service on terms no less favourable than those set out herein, as contemplated in the relief sought in the Reply.

 

1.3 CN’s Answer states, at paragraph 24, that “at no point has CN refused to service Power Wood” and that CN has “proposed alternatives to mitigate” capacity constraints. On that pleading, the question before the Agency is not whether a service obligation exists but whether the conditions CN has attached to performance — including the Pickardville capitalDemand contribution referred todefined below — are commercially fair and reasonable within the meaning of s. 112 CTA. TheThis Framework is drafted to allow that question to be answered on a concrete and orderable record.

 

 

2. Background and Basis

 

2.1 The Project. Power Wood is developing a black pellet manufacturing facility near La Crête, Alberta. The Traffic (black pellets, bulk density approximately 750 kg/m³) moves by truck from La Crête to a dedicated transload at High Level, Alberta, and from High Level by covered hopper unit train on CN’s Mackenzie Northern Railway (the “MKNR”) to the Port of Prince Rupert for export to Japanese electricity generators.

 

2.2 Circularity. Power Wood cannot close its construction financing without a CN service commitment on lender-bankable terms. CN has to date declined to commit because it alleges Power Wood has not yet tendered actual traffic. Power Wood has given estimates, but cannot tender actual traffic, because, without a service commitment, it cannot close financing and build. TheThis Framework resolves that mutual dependence by synchronizing both parties’ obligations on the parallel conditions precedent in paragraph 8.

 

2.3 2019–2024 engagement. Between 2019 and 2024, CN actively participated in shaping the commercial and operational parameters now reflected in this Framework. Without limitation: (a) CN recommended Mr. Greg Karpo (a 37-year CN operations veteran, now an independent rail consultant) to Power Wood as the expert to design the unit train transload concept at High Level; (b) CN recommended shifting the export routing from the Port of Vancouver to the Port of Prince Rupert, which Power Wood adopted on CN’s advice; (c) CN contributed to the design of the High Level unit train loop and issued the design parameters against which Power Wood’s approximately $11 million track and transload capital commitment was structured; (d) on March 18, 2024, CN advised that it could accommodate an initial service level of approximately 20 covered hopper cars per week on the MKNR; and (e) on or about April 17, 2024, CN declined to provide the service commitment requested and conditioned further engagement on a capital contribution by Power Wood (estimated at approximately $53 million) toward aan proposedupgrade sidingof rail infrastructure at Pickardvillevarious locations including, Albertabut not limited to, McLennan Yard and Pickardville (the “Pickardville Demand”).

 

2.4 CN prior positions incorporated. The following positions taken by CN during the 2019–2024 engagement are incorporated into this Framework as the operational spine of the arrangement: (a) a Phase 1 operational level of approximately 20 cars per week (CN’s March 18, 2024 position); (b) the Port of Prince Rupert as destination (CN’s routing recommendation); (c) the design parameters of the High Level unit train loop and transload (CN’s engineering input); and (d) the covered hopper car configuration and Manning Subdivision operating assumptions (CN’s operational input). None of the foregoing is a Power Wood demand on CN.

 

2.5 Karpo Report findings. The Rail Expert Opinion Report of Mr. Greg Karpo dated December 29, 2025 (the “Karpo Report”), filed as Appendix A to the Application, establishes that the MKNR has sufficient existing operational capacity to accommodate Power Wood’s Phase 2 full-capacity service (approximately 36 unit trains per year, an approximately 3.8% increase in corridor traffic relative to 2024 traffic) with only incremental crew and locomotive positioning and without any new siding or other major capital upgrade. MKNR corridor volumes have declined since 2021, and the Phase 2 volume increase sits within CN’s own stated corridor growth envelope.

 

2.6 Power Wood’s sunk commitments. Power Wood has incurred approximately $35 million in pre-construction expenditures (land, permits, front-end engineering and design, and professional fees), is approximately 70–80% through front-end engineering and design with Valmet, SNC Lavalin/AtkinsRealis, and WB Melback, holds letters of intent for off-take arrangements with Sumitomo Corporation and additional Japanese counterparties covering aggregate committed volumes at or above Phase 2 full capacity, and has construction financing committed in principle at approximately $150 million under letters of intent, subject to conditions precedent that include a CN service commitment on lender-bankable terms.

3. CN Service Commitment

 

3.1 Core commitment. CN will, during the Service Period, furnish adequate and suitable accommodation for the receiving, loading, carriage, and delivery of the Traffic in covered hopper cars of minimum 4,850 cubic feet capacity from the High Level transload to the Port of Prince Rupert, in accordance with ss. 113–114 CTA, the Volume Schedule in paragraph 3.2, and the Service Reliability KPIs in paragraph 3.4.

 

3.2 Volume Schedule. Commencing on the Commencement Date, CN will accommodate the Traffic on the following phased basis:

 

(a) Commissioning Period (Months 1–3): approximately 10 cars per week, operated as partial train sets by accommodation of CN, with flexible scheduling matched to Power Wood’s actual production output; KPI measurement for baseline purposes only.

(c) Phase 2 (Month 25 onwards): approximately 77 carsUnit Trains per week,year (approximately one every 10 days), approximately 3,813 carloads per year, andat approximately 36 Unit Trains per year (approximately 105 cars per Unit Train)..

For the avoidance of doubt, following the Commissioning Period the Volume Schedule is expressed and measured in Unit Trains; service level adjustments shall be made by changing Unit Train cadence (for example, from one Unit Train per month to one Unit Train every 10 days) and not by tendering partial train sets.

3.3 Car supply. Primary Position: CN will supply the covered hopper cars required for the Traffic as part of its service obligation, with the ordinary capital and maintenance cost of the car pool borne by CN and reflected in the Freight Rate. CN will maintain a dedicated car pool sufficient to meet the Volume Schedule at each operational phase. Alternative Position: if the parties agree that a private car pool structure is commercially preferable, Power Wood will lease or acquire a dedicated fleet of covered hopper cars, tender those cars to CN for movement, and pay a reduced Freight Rate. Consequences of failure. If CN fails to meet any KPI in a measurement period, Power Wood’s take-or-pay obligation is reduced proportionally by the volumes affected. If performance on a metric falls below 90% of target for two consecutive quarters, Power Wood is entitled to a Freight Rate credit equal to 10% of the variable cost component of the affected movements. Performance below 85% of target for four consecutive quarters constitutes a material breach entitling Power Wood to terminate this Framework without penalty and to resumepursue all damages and all remedies available at law or in equity, including (without limitation) all civil remedies and statutory remedies under the CTA remedies.

 

3.5 Advance notice of operational constraints. CN will provide not less than 90 days’ prior written notice of any planned maintenance, capacity constraint, or other circumstance reasonably anticipated to affect its ability to meet its obligations, (a “Constraint Period”), together with the nature and anticipated duration of the constraint, the portion of the Volume Schedule or KPIs affected, and CN’s proposed mitigation plan. If the anticipated operational constraint is expected to last more than 7 days, CN will provide not less than 120 days’ prior written notice. During any such noticeConstraint period, Power Wood’s take-or-pay obligation is suspended to the extent of the affected volumes.

 

 

4. Power Wood Volume, Take-or-Pay, and Security Commitments

 

4.1 Take-or-pay obligation. Power Wood will tender, or pay CN for the equivalent of, the following minimum annual carload volumes during each year of the Service Period: (a) during the Commissioning Period, no minimum applies; (b) during Phase 1, 80% of the applicable ramping Volume Schedule; and (c) during Phase 2, 80% of the Phase 2 Volume Schedule (approximately 3,050 carloads per year).

 

4.2 Two-tier structure. For any shortfall between the take-or-pay floor and carloads actually tendered, Power Wood will pay CN a reservation fee equal to [NTD: confirm — proposed 50%] of the variable cost component of the Freight Rate applied to the shortfall carloads, reflecting CN’s reserved-capacity cost on cars not tendered. For carloads actually tendered and moved, Power Wood will pay the full Freight Rate. Shortfall is calculated quarterly with a year-end true-up.

 

4.3 Suspension events. The take-or-pay obligation is suspended during: (a) any failure by CN to meet a Service Reliability KPI, to the extent of the affected volumes; (b) any period covered by a CN advance notice of operational constraint; (c) Force Majeure; (d) any period in which Power Wood is unable to tender Traffic by reason of an insured plant disruption, with the insurance proceeds satisfying the obligation to the extent of the insured period; and (e) any material default by CN.

 

4.4 Notice obligations. Power Wood will provide CN with not less than (a) 24 months’ notice of Financial Close and[NTD: confirm 24-month period with the construction completion schedule;business]; (b) 12 months’ notice of the Phase 2 ramp-up, with a preliminary monthly volume forecast; (c) 60 days’ rolling monthly volume requirements before each calendar month; and (d) 30 days’ confirmed loading date and car order before each Unit Train.

 

4.5 Letter of credit security. Power Wood will maintain, in favour of CN from the Commencement Date throughout the Service Period, a renewable standby letter of credit (the “L/C”) issued by a Canadian Schedule I chartered bank (or another issuer rated not less than A‑/A3 acceptable to CN), in an amount equal to three months of the Phase 2 take-or-pay reservation fee exposure (estimated at approximately $5.1 million and scaling down for the Commissioning Period and Phase 1). The L/C renews automatically for successive twelve-month periods unless Power Wood delivers not less than 60 days’ prior written notice of non-renewal (any such non-renewal being an event of default if take-or-pay obligations remain outstanding). CN may draw on the L/C to satisfy any confirmed quarterly shortfall Power Wood has failed to pay within 30 days of invoice, on 10 business days’ prior written notice of intent to draw during which Power Wood may dispute the draw under paragraph 10.

4.6 Power Wood infrastructure commitment. Power Wood will construct, at its cost and from its construction financing, the unit train loop, transload, and loading infrastructure at High Level, at an estimated cost of approximately $11 million, in accordance with the design parameters reflected in the CN Prior Positions. The infrastructure will be completed prior to the Commencement Date. The High Level unit train loop, transload, and loading infrastructure are for Power Wood’s exclusive use, and CN shall not use, permit the use of, or allocate capacity on that infrastructure to any third party without Power Wood’s prior written consent.

 

 

5. Capital Expenditure Allocation

 

5.1 Principles. Capital expenditure in connection with the service under this Framework is allocated on the following principles, each an element of commercial reasonableness under s. 112 CTA: (a) Power Wood bears the cost of infrastructure specific to its own operations (the La Crête facility, the High Level unit train loop, and the High Level transload); (b) CN bears the cost of corridor infrastructure, recoverable through standard rates; (c) no capital expenditure is required of either party unless operationally necessary for the service level then in effect; and (d) where any CN expenditure both serves the Traffic and benefits other MKNR users, Power Wood’s contribution is limited to its documented pro-rata share of the incremental benefit Power Wood receives.

 

5.2 Phase 1 — no CN capital contribution required. On the findings of the Karpo Report, and on CN’s own March 18, 2024 position that it could accommodate approximately 20 cars per week within existing infrastructure, no CN capital expenditure is operationally required for the Commissioning Period or Phase 1. Power Wood does not accept, and will not contribute to, any CN capital expenditure as a condition of Commissioning Period or Phase 1 service.

 

5.3 Phase 2 — contingent and contested. Primary Position: on the Karpo Report findings, no CN capital expenditure is operationally required for Phase 2 either. A 3.8% corridor traffic increase does not trigger a capacity constraint requiring new infrastructure and sits within CN’s stated growth envelope on a corridor where volumes have declined since 2021. Alternative Position: if, through the dispute process in paragraph 10, the parties agree or an arbitral body determines that specific CN capital expenditure is operationally necessary for Phase 2, Power Wood will contribute only on the basis that (i) the item is specifically and uniquely required for the Traffic and is not part of CN’s ordinary network investment program, (ii) Power Wood’s contribution is limited to its documented pro-rata share of incremental benefit, (iii) CN demonstrates the expenditure is not recoverable through ordinary tariff mechanisms or contributions from other beneficiaries, and (iv) any contribution is subject to the recoupment in paragraph 5.5.

 

5.4 Pickardville Demand — rejected as a blanket precondition. Power Wood does not accept the Pickardville Demand as a precondition to service. The proposed siding is located approximately 460 miles south of High Level, would benefit all existing MKNR customers rather than Power Wood specifically, is not operationally required on the Karpo Report findings, and, on the revenues and margins implied by the Freight Rate applied to Phase 2 volumes, would generate a return to CN exceeding CN’s publicly stated corporate return-on-invested-capital target. The Pickardville Demand therefore reflects a business decision, not a genuine operational or financial obstacle to service.

 

5.5 Third-party benefit recoupment. To the extent any CN capital expenditure is ultimately contributed to by Power Wood under paragraph 5.3 and generates operational benefits for other MKNR users, Power Wood is entitled, at CNPower Wood’s election, to recoupment on a pro-rata basis by (a) a rate rebate applied to Freight Rate invoices over a defined payback period, (b) a direct cash offset against Power Wood’s contribution to the extent CN recovers cost from other users, or (c) a first-mover benefit credit recognizing the capacity Power Wood’s contribution creates.

8. Term and Parallel Conditions Precedent

 

8.1 Initial Term. The Service Period runs for an initial term of 10 years from the Commencement Date, with good-faith renewal discussions commencing not later than 24 months before expiry. [NTD: Confirm with lenders whether 10 years is sufficient for debt amortization, or whether a 12- to 15-year term is required.]

 

8.2 Parallel conditionality. The commercial arrangement contemplated by this Framework is one of mutual dependence: Power Wood cannot perform without the service commitment from CN, and CN’s service commitment is not required to be performed until Power Wood is ready to tender Traffic. The Framework resolves that mutual dependence by synchronizing both parties’ obligations on the parallel conditions precedent in paragraphs 8.3 and 8.4. Each is objective and commercially defined; none of them confers on either party a discretion to reopen the commercial terms of this Framework at commencement.

9. Lender Alignment

 

9.1 Lender Direct Agreement. CN acknowledges that Power Wood’s construction and term financing requires the Framework to be enforceable by and assignable to Power Wood’s lenders on a project finance basis. Concurrently with Power Wood’s Financial Close, CN will enter into a tripartite direct agreement (the “Lender Direct Agreement”) with Power Wood and Power Wood’s lenders (or an administrative agent on their behalf), containing:

 

(a) Step-in rights permitting the lenders, upon an event of default by Power Wood under the financing documents, to step into and continue Power Wood’s performance of this Framework directly or through a substitute operator reasonably acceptable to CN;

 

(b) Cure rights permitting the lenders not less than 30 days’ notice and opportunity to cure any Power Wood default under this Framework before CN may exercise termination remedies;

 

(c) Assignment to substitute operator, permitting the lenders to assign Power Wood’s rights and obligations to a substitute operator without further CN consent, provided the substitute operator is financially and operationally capable of performance and has expressly assumed Power Wood’s obligations in writing;

 

(d) No termination for insolvency, precluding CN from terminating this Framework solely by reason of Power Wood’s insolvency, bankruptcy, or restructuring, provided the lenders or a substitute operator continue performance; and

 

(e) Information rights in respect of material notices under this Framework.

 

Delivery of the executed Lender Direct Agreement is a condition precedent to Financial Close and, accordingly, to the operative effect of Power Wood’s commitments under this Framework.

10. Governance and Dispute Resolution

 

10.1 Joint Operational Committee. Within 60 days of the trigger of the parallel conditions precedent, the parties will establish a Joint Operational Committee (the “JOC”) of two representatives per party, meeting not less than quarterly, with scope covering operational forecasting, car supply planning, KPI review, safety and emergency coordination, and first-tier resolution of operational disputes.

 

10.2 Tiered dispute resolution. Any dispute arising under this Framework will be resolved through: (a) Tier 1 — referral to the JOC; (b) Tier 2 — escalation to senior executives of CN (not below Vice-President, Commercial) and Power Wood (not below Chief Executive Officer) for 30 days of good-faith discussion; (c) Tier 3 — Expert determination. For any dispute that is technical, operational, or valuation-related in nature (including KPI measurement disputes, capital cost allocation disputes, and Freight Rate review disputes), either party may refer the matter to expert determination by an independent expert jointly appointed by the parties (or, failing agreement, appointed by [NTD: appointing authority to confirm]), whose determination shall be final and binding on the referred issue; and (c) Tier 3 — arbitration under s. 169.31 CTA or any other dispute mechanism available under the CTA at the relevant time. Neither party loses any rights under the CTA as a consequence of participating in this process.
